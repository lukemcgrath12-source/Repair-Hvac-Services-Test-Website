  • Use the icon without the wordmark in most contexts

Background
Navy — Primary Use
Used for headlines, body text, buttons, navigation, and footer backgrounds. The dominant colour across all digital and print materials.
Teal — Accent Use
Used for CTAs, links, icons, highlight bars, and active states. Draws attention to key actions. Never use as a background on large areas in print.
White — Neutral Use
Primary background colour. Keeps layouts clean and breathable. Pairs with navy text for maximum legibility across all contexts.

04

Voice & Tone

Straight-up honest
We say what the job actually needs. No upsells, no scare tactics. Customers get options and they choose. Our copy should feel the same way.
"Your unit needs a clean and a regas — we'll run you through both options."
Reliable, not robotic
We're real people, not a call centre. Warm and approachable, but always professional. Write like a tradie who genuinely gives a damn.
"We'll keep you in the loop from the first call to the final check."
Knowledgeable, not showy
17+ combined years in the trade. We know HVAC inside out — but we explain things simply. No jargon for the sake of sounding technical.
"We install Mitsubishi Electric and Daikin — two of the best units on the market."
Available when it counts
After hours, weekends — we show up. Our availability is a real differentiator. Mention it plainly and let it speak for itself.
"Available evenings and weekends — call us when it suits you."
